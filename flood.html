Flood Awareness

Learn how to prepare and stay safe during a flood

Overview

Flooding is the overflow of water onto normally dry land. It may result from heavy rainfall, storm surges, river overflow, or the failure of structures like dams. Floods can develop quickly (flash floods) or slowly over several days, causing major damage to infrastructure and communities.

Causes

  • Heavy and prolonged rainfall
  • River overflow
  • Storm surge from typhoons
  • Dam failure
  • Poor drainage systems

Effects

  • Property and infrastructure damage
  • Water contamination
  • Displacement of communities
  • Injury and loss of life
  • Crop damage leading to food shortages

Warning Signs

  • Heavy, continuous rain
  • Rising water levels in rivers or streets
  • Government flood advisories
  • Storm surge during typhoons

Safety Tips

Before

  • Know evacuation routes
  • Keep valuables on higher floors
  • Prepare an emergency kit
  • Listen to government advisories

During

  • Evacuate immediately if advised
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters
  • Move to higher ground
  • Stay updated via radio/news

After

  • Avoid floodwater — risk of contamination & electrocution
  • Check for structural damage before entering buildings
  • Boil drinking water or use purification tablets
  • Follow official safety announcements

Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Water & food (3 days minimum)
  • Waterproof storage containers
  • Clothing & sturdy boots
  • Flashlight & batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Water purification tablets
  • Whistle

Real-Life Example

The 2010 Pakistan floods affected around 20 million people and caused widespread destruction to homes, agriculture, and infrastructure. It was one of the worst floods in modern history.
