Typhoon

Overview

A typhoon is a tropical cyclone characterized by extremely strong winds, heavy rain, and storm surges. The Philippines experiences many typhoons each year due to its location along the Pacific typhoon belt.

Causes

  • Warm ocean waters
  • Low-pressure atmospheric systems
  • Interaction of humidity and rotating wind currents

Effects

  • Strong wind damage to structures
  • Flooding and heavy rainfall
  • Storm surges in coastal areas
  • Power outages and communication loss

Warning Signs

  • Weather advisories from PAGASA
  • Rising wind speeds and dark clouds
  • Decreasing atmospheric pressure

Safety Tips

Before

  • Secure windows and outdoor items
  • Prepare an evacuation plan
  • Stock food, water, and essentials

During

  • Stay indoors and away from windows
  • Avoid coastal areas
  • Monitor official weather reports

After

  • Avoid floodwaters
  • Check structural safety of buildings
  • Boil water before drinking

Preparedness Kit

  • Battery-powered radio
  • Waterproof bag for documents
  • 3-day supply of water & ready-to-eat food
  • Extra batteries and flashlights
  • Tarps and rope

Real-life Example

Typhoon Haiyan (Yolanda), 2013 — One of the strongest tropical cyclones in history devastated parts of the Philippines, causing massive storm surges particularly in Tacloban, claiming more than 6,000 lives.
