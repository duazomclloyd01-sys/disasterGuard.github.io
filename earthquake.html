Earthquake Awareness

Learn earthquake causes, warning signs, and safety tips.

Overview

An earthquake is the shaking of the ground caused by the sudden movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. The intensity and duration can vary, causing damage to buildings, infrastructure, and sometimes tsunamis if undersea.

Causes

  • Tectonic plate movement
  • Fault slips
  • Volcanic activity
  • Human-induced events (rare)

Effects

  • Building collapse
  • Landslides
  • Infrastructure damage
  • Tsunamis (if undersea)

Warning Signs

  • Little to none — most earthquakes come without warning
  • Small foreshocks may occur before major shockwaves

Safety Tips

Before

  • Secure heavy furniture & appliances
  • Create a disaster plan & meeting point
  • Prepare an emergency kit

During

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  • Stay away from windows & fragile items
  • If outdoors: move to an open area

After

  • Expect aftershocks
  • Check gas lines and damaged structures
  • Evacuate if necessary

Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Hard hat
  • Sturdy shoes
  • Flashlight & batteries
  • Water & non-perishable food
  • Whistle & local maps

Real-Life Example

The 1990 Luzon earthquake in the Philippines caused widespread destruction, particularly in Baguio City and surrounding areas, resulting in thousands of casualties and extensive damage to buildings and infrastructure.
