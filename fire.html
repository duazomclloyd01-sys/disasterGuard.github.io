Fire

Uncontrolled fire can cause injuries, property loss, and widespread damage. Knowing how fires start and what to do can save lives.

Description / Overview

Fire is the rapid oxidation of a material in the exothermic chemical process of combustion, releasing heat, light and various reaction products.

Causes

  • Unattended cooking
  • Faulty electrical wiring or appliances
  • Open flames (candles, stoves)
  • Chemicals and accelerants
  • Wildfires caused by dry weather and sparks

Effects

  • Burn injuries and smoke inhalation
  • Loss of life and property
  • Infrastructure damage (power, transport)
  • Air pollution and long-term health effects

Warning Signs

  • Unusual smell of smoke
  • Visible flames or embers
  • Electrical sparks or burning plastic smell
  • Alarms sounding (smoke detector)

Safety Tips

Before

  • Install and test smoke detectors monthly.
  • Plan and practice escape routes with family.
  • Have a fire extinguisher and know how to use it.
  • Keep flammable items away from heat sources.

During

  • Get low to avoid smoke; crawl if necessary.
  • Use the nearest safe exit; do not use elevators.
  • If trapped, close doors and seal gaps, signal from a window.
  • If clothing catches fire — stop, drop, and roll.

After

  • Call emergency services once safe.
  • Do not re-enter the structure until cleared.
  • Seek medical help for smoke inhalation or burns.
  • Contact your insurance provider and document damage.

Preparedness Kit (Fire-focused)

  • Battery-powered flashlight + extra batteries
  • Smoke alarms and spare batteries
  • Multi-purpose (ABC) fire extinguisher
  • First-aid kit with burn dressings
  • Emergency contact list & important documents (copies)
  • Whistle to signal for help

Real-life Examples

  • Household fires: Kitchen fires from unattended cooking causing major losses.
  • Wildfires: Large-scale bushfires destroying communities during drought / wind events.
