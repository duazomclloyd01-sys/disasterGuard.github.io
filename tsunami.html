Tsunami Awareness

Prepare, stay alert, and learn lifesaving safety measures.

Overview

A tsunami is a series of powerful ocean waves usually caused by underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides. These waves can travel extremely fast and rise to great heights upon reaching coastal areas, causing widespread destruction.

Causes

  • Underwater earthquakes (most common)
  • Volcanic eruptions
  • Submarine landslides
  • Meteor impacts (rare)

Effects

  • Massive coastal flooding
  • Destruction of homes and buildings
  • Loss of life
  • Saltwater intrusion into freshwater systems
  • Damage to infrastructure

Warning Signs

  • Strong or long-lasting earthquake felt near coastal areas
  • Ocean water suddenly recedes or rises unusually
  • Loud roaring sound coming from the ocean
  • Official tsunami alerts and sirens

Safety Tips

Before

  • Learn and practice evacuation routes
  • Know the location of high ground
  • Prepare an emergency kit

During

  • Move to high ground immediately
  • Do NOT wait to see the waves
  • Stay away from coastal areas

After

  • Stay away from flooded areas
  • Expect multiple waves
  • Wait for official “all clear” notice

Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Water (at least 3 days)
  • Non-perishable food
  • Whistle
  • First aid kit
  • Flashlight & batteries
  • Life jackets (if available)
  • Maps of evacuation areas

Real-Life Example

The 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami, triggered by a magnitude 9.1 earthquake, caused catastrophic damage across multiple countries and resulted in over 200,000 deaths.
